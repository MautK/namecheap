I could have been at the beach right now

During the walk along and the research on the topographical border of the Netherlands for the first part of I could have been at the beach right now, I realised more and more that in the Netherlands it is not the topographical border that determines the future shore line, in stead I had to look at artificially made borders – political borders. In the centuries long fights of the Dutch against the water, they build all kinds of water defence structures like dikes, storm barriers, locks, sluices and dams. Because of the enormous costs of updating and maintaining them, choices need to be taking in terms of prioritizing certain parts of the country. In the Netherlands this is done by assigning priorities to the ‘Dijjkringen’. In the summer of 2019, I walked Dijkring 14, the ring around the major cities and economical centres, therefore naturally the ring with the highest priority, the see how the prioritized land looks like.
